What problem are we solving?

Problem statement

In Niger, women face significant challenges in accessing education and equal opportunities due to factors such as poverty, early marriages, and entrenched patriarchy. According to Girls not Brides, 76% of girls in Niger are married before they turn 18, and 28% are married before the age of 15. These barriers contribute to low female school enrollment and retention rates. Niger is one of the poorest countries in the world, being second from bottom on the current UN Human Development Index. Food shortages, a harsh natural environment, and frequent droughts drive some families to marry off their daughters to men of wealth as a survival tactic, robbing girls of their childhood and educational prospects. In addition, many child marriages are arranged without the girls' consent, hence emphasizing their expected role as wives. Pressure to marry is high, as disobedience is believed to bring curses and marriage is seen as a safeguard against perceived promiscuity of teenage girls and potential family shame due to pregnancies out of marriage. These issues necessitate immediate attention and targeted interventions to empower women and create a more equitable society in Niger.

Our mission

Our mission is to empower women in Niger through education and community initiatives. We aim to address poverty, early marriages, and patriarchy by offering vocational training, providing sexual education for men, and enhancing teacher training. We envision a future where every girl in Niger has the opportunity to thrive by 2026.

What Makes us special?

Solution statement

We create a safe space for girls aged 12 to 16 through our specialized boarding schools, providing an environment for uninterrupted learning and personal growth. We offer comprehensive life skills training and ensure active parent engagement to foster a supportive community. Graduates receive priority access to scholarships, enabling them to pursue higher education and career aspirations. With a balanced curriculum that includes physical education, arts, and extracurricular activities, we aim to empower young women, equipping them with the skills and opportunities needed to thrive and make meaningful contributions to their communities.
